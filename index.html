A DOGE staffer broke Treasury policy by emailing unencrypted personal data

A Treasury staffer, Marko Elez, violated policy by emailing a spreadsheet with transaction data to two Trump administration officials without encryption or approval. Elez, a former SpaceX employee, resigned in February over racist social media posts but was later rehired at the Social Security Administration. The Treasury claims he did not alter payment systems, but a lawsuit by 19 state attorneys general argues DOGE’s access was mishandled. The court has restricted DOGE’s access, and the government is seeking to modify the order. Critics say the findings highlight a rushed and chaotic onboarding process.

Today's Frequently Asked Question...

What is a "supply chain attack," and why is it dangerous?

A supply chain attack compromises software, hardware, or services before they reach the end user. Attackers inject malicious code into trusted updates, developer tools, or hardware components, leading to a widespread attack (e.g., SolarWinds attack).

What is the primary function of a honeypot in cybersecurity?

To encrypt sensitive information before transmission
To authenticate users before granting network access
To detect and study cyberattacks by luring attackers into a controlled environment
To act as a backup firewall in case of primary firewall failure
